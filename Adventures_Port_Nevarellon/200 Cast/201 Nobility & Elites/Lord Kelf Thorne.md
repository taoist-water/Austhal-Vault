# Character: Lord Kelf Thorne
#cast/active #status/solid

> "I am not an exile. I am the Baron of the Silt. The High Quarter chokes on its own laws; here, I breathe free air and take a cut of the tide." — Lord Thorne

## 📊 Vital Statistics
- **Full Name / Aliases:** Lord Kelf Thorne / "The Mud Baron"
- **Current Occupation:** Self-Proclaimed Baron of the Silted Marshes / Syndicate Boss of Divtown.
- **Social Class / Standing:** Disgraced Nobility / Underworld Aristocrat.
- **Primary Residence:** The "Manor"—a heavily fortified, multi-story timber longhouse built on the most stable mud-bank in Divtown.
- **Affiliations:** House Thorne (Disowned); The Grey Water Pirates (Fencer and Liaison).

## ⚖️ Realism & Physicality
- **Age & Vitality:** 45 years old. He desperately tries to maintain an air of highborn grace, but the marsh has taken its toll. He suffers from a chronic, wet cough from the humidity and his skin is sallow and pale.
- **Physical Flaws / Limitations:** He refuses to wear practical boots, insisting on fine leather shoes that are perpetually ruined by the mud. He is not a fighter; his hands are soft and uncalloused. 
- **Financial Status:** Wealthy in stolen goods and raw timber, but unable to spend his wealth in polite society. His power relies entirely on maintaining the bureaucratic illusion that his noble wax seal is still legally binding in Port Nevarellon.
- **Equipment & Upkeep:** Wears outdated, faded silk doublets from a decade ago, layered under heavy, practical marsh-cloaks. Carries a rusted, ceremonial rapier that has likely never been drawn in anger.

## 🧠 Psychology & Drive
- **Immediate Goal:** Keep the Golden Company and the [[The Iron-Anchor Syndicate|Iron-Anchor Syndicate]] from realizing exactly how much untaxed wealth he is pulling out of the Grey Water Lagoon.
- **The Core Fear:** Being dragged back to [[The High Quarter|The High Quarter]] to face trial for his past debts and crimes, which would strip him of his remaining illusion of nobility.
- **Moral Compromises:** Kelf presents himself as a savior to the escaped Chalced slaves and outcasts, but in reality, he is running a feudal crime syndicate. He exploits their labor in the dangerous timber swamps, knowing they have nowhere else to go.
