### Magic Systems

Magic is raw cosmic radiation channelled through a mortal nervous system. 

**The Danger (Overcasting):** A mage can cast spells all day long... as long as they manage their Stress. If a spell's cost pushes them over their Maximum Stress Limit, the excess Stress immediately converts into physical Wounds (burst blood vessels, mystical burns, shattered bones). 

*Arcane Casters* require a [[Grimoire]] of spells as an equipment item to cast spells without additional penalties.

*Divine/Faith Casters* require a holy relic/symbol of their deity/faith

##  The Laws of Arcana (Wits)

Arcane magic is a science of chaotic energy. It requires absolute mental precision, physical gesturing, and the complex formulas recorded in a Grimoire.

- The Cost (Upfront Heat): Every time an Arcanist casts a non-cantrip spell, they immediately suffer its cost in [[Locked Stress]] (1 for Novice, 2 for Adept, 3 for Master). This is an absolute transaction.
    
- *The Cast (The Check):* Inside or outside of combat, the Arcanist rolls 2d6 + Wits + Arcana vs. [[TN ]]8 (or opposed).
    
- *Success:* The spell manifests.
    
- *Massive Success (12+):* The spell manifests, and you generate 1 Spell Momentum.
    
- Fumble:* The spell fails, and the chaotic energy backfires. You take the standard 3 Dissonant Stress, and all your current Locked Stress immediately converts into [[Dissonant Stress]].
    
- *No Maintenance:* Once an Arcane spell is cast, it persists for its duration without requiring any further rolls or actions. The Arcanist can cast multiple spells, stacking effects, as long as they have the Stress Limit to handle the Locked Stress.
    
- *The Grounding Valve:* Because Locked Stress doesn't clear on its own, the Arcanist must actively use an Action to perform Grounding. If they push the engine too hard and exceed their Stress Limit, their nervous system shatters, and the excess converts to Wounds.
    
- *The Grimoire:* To cast any Novice, Adept, Master Arcana spell, the caster must have their Grimoire open and in one of their hands.
    

- *Grimoire Casting:*  Arcane caster cast their spells from their open Spell book [[Grimoire]] tracing the complex geometries and speaking the formulas out loud.  The act of casting and concentration causes stress to the caster.  Spells all have an associated Stress cost.  This is the cost of [[Locked Stress]] to cast the spell.
    
- *Grounding:* An Arcanist can attempt to vent the magical heat in their mind. Roll Wits + Arcana (TN 8).  Success: Unlock *Wits* amount of Stress (Min 1). High Success (12+): Clear all Locked Stress. Fumble: The energy 'leaks'—all Locked Stress becomes Dissonant.
    
- *Blind Casting:* If an Arcane caster is disarmed, bound, or has their Grimoire stowed, they can attempt to cast from raw memory. This is incredibly dangerous. Blind Casting, the casting check is made at Disadvantage.
    

- *Somatic (Gestures):* Arcana requires at least one hand completely free to weave the physical energy. If a mage is Grappled or holding a weapon/shield in both hands, they cannot cast Arcana.
    
- *Verbal (Incantations):* The formulas must be spoken aloud. If the mage is gagged, underwater, or in a Zone of Silence, Arcana casting is impossible.
    

## The Limit of Cantrips

### Cantrip Limitation: 
- Cantrips are raw, unrefined magical tricks. Because of their simplistic nature, they cannot be enhanced with Momentum, Power Modifiers, or Feats that increase Impact or Area of Effect. What you see is what you get.


## The Grimoire: Equipment Profile

A Grimoire is a heavy, complex tome of arcane mathematics and geometry.

- Grip: 1H (Occupies one hand while open and in use).
    
- Tags: Arcane Focus, Repository.
    

### The Mechanics of the Grimoire

#### The Repository Rule (Storage)

Cantrips are simple enough to be committed entirely to muscle memory. However, Novice, Adept and Master spells are incredibly complex. An Arcane Caster can only cast  spells that are actively written in their Grimoire.

- If the book is stolen, burned, or lost, the Arcane Caster is reduced to Cantrips until they can spend downtime scribing a new one (or they must rely on their Signature Mastery spell if they are a high-level character).
    

#### Active Casting vs. Blind Casting 

To cast a non-Cantrip spell, the Arcane Caster must reference the complex formulas.

- *Open Book (1 Hand Occupied):*  The standard way to cast. The Arcane Caster holds the book in one hand and gestures with the other.
    

- *Benefit:* Standard casting rules apply. The book acts as a mental anchor.
    

- *Blind Casting (Book Stowed / Hands Full):* If the Arcane Caster has put the book away to hold a sword and shield, or is hanging onto a cliff face, they can try to cast a non-Cantrip spell purely from memory.
    

- *Penalty:* The Mage suffers Disadvantage (3d6 keep lowest) on the Arcana roll, as their brain strains to visualize the complex geometry without the visual aid.

## The Laws of Faith (Will)

Faith magic does not rely on written formulas; the power comes from absolute belief and divine connection. It relies on scripture spoken aloud and a physical anchor to reality.

- *The Relic:* A Faith caster does not need a book, but they must possess a physical Relic or Holy Symbol. It is required to ground the divine energy during sustained miracles.
    

- *Shattered Focus:* A Faith caster must be touching or holding their Relic to cast or maintain a spell with the Channelling tag. If an enemy disarms them or forces them to drop the Relic while a spell is active, the spell instantly drops, and the sudden severance inflicts 2 Dissonant Stress.
    

- *The Cost (The Toll of Channelling):* Faith spells cost 0 Stress upfront. However, every non-instantaneous Faith spell gains the Channelling tag.
    
- *The Cast (The Check):* Inside or outside of combat, the Cleric rolls 2d6 + Will + Faith vs. TN 8 (or opposed).
    

- *Success:* The miracle manifests.
    
- *Massive Success (12+):* The miracle manifests, and you generate 1 Spell Momentum.
    
- *Fumble:* The deity rejects the call, or the mortal shell recoils. The caster suffers 3 Dissonant Stress.
    

- *The Maintenance (The Burden):* At the start of the caster's turn, they must roll a Will + Faith check against TN 8 to maintain the Channelling effect.
    

- *Pass:* The spell persists.
    
- *Fail:* The spell persists, but the sheer weight of the divine energy crushes their mind. They instantly suffer 1 Dissonant Stress.
    
- *Relic Disarmed:* If the caster loses their Relic, the spell instantly shatters, and the sudden severance inflicts 2 Dissonant Stress.
    

- *Verbal (Scripture/Prayer):* Faith is fundamentally a spoken pact. A Cleric must be able to loudly proclaim their prayers. Like Arcana, Faith cannot be cast if the user is silenced.
    
- *Somatic (Minimal):* Unlike Arcana, Faith does not require complex weaving. A Cleric can cast a Faith spell even while holding a mace and a heavy shield, or while grappled, provided they can still clutch their Relic and speak.
    

### The Faith Implement: Relics & Holy Symbols

While Arcane spells are formulas stored in a book, Faith spells are convictions channelled from a higher power. A Faith caster doesn't need to read, but they do need a physical anchor to safely ground that divine energy.

- *The Relic / Holy Symbol (1H):* A physical totem of their deity or oath.
    
- *Unmoored Channelling (Blind Casting equivalent):* If the caster is disarmed, or puts the Relic away to use a two-handed weapon, they can still cast spells (their faith is in their heart, not the item). However, without the physical anchor to ground the divine energy, any failure on a Channelling roll inflicts 2 Dissonant Stress instead of 1, and they suffer Disadvantage on the initial casting roll.
