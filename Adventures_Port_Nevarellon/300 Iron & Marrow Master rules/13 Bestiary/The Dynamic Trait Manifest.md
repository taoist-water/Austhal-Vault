# The Dynamic Trait Manifest
Design Philosophy: Keep stat blocks microscopic. Let traits dictate tactical behavior, stress interaction, and momentum usage.

###  Core Integration Rules

- Fodder: Max 1 Trait. Fodder cannot spend Momentum unless led by a leader.
    
- Elites: 1–2 Traits. Start with 1 Momentum banked.
    
- Bosses: 2–4 Traits. Start with 3 Momentum banked and can use Reactionary Traits.
    

---

##  DEFENSIVE & PHYSIOLOGICAL TRAITS

### Hardy 

- Mechanical Translation: This creature's physical resilience acts as a natural buffer against injury.
    
- 2d6 Application: Increase the creature’s Stress Limit by +2. Additionally, can spend Momentum to Mitigate damage, it reduces the incoming Impact by 2 per point spent.
    
- Dev Note: Put this on frontline brutes, or undead to make players burning through their health pool feel the attrition.
    

### Plated 
- Mechanical Translation: Thick hide, rusted iron carapace, or heavy plate scales shield vital locations.
    
- 2d6 Application: Reduces all incoming standard Impact damage by a flat -1. However, if a player achieves Perfect Form (rolling a natural 3 and 4 on their Clash check) or triggers an Ace (exploding 6), this armor is bypassed for that attack.
    

### Skittering 

- Mechanical Translation: Unnatural speed, shifting limbs, or erratic reflexes make them slippery targets.
    
- 2d6 Application: When this creature acts as a Reactor in a Clash and wins the roll, it can immediately execute a Free Shift (move 1 zone/square) without triggering counter-actions. Furthermore, they receive a +1 bonus to all Draw (Initiative) checks.
    

---

##  OFFENSIVE & MARTIAL TRAITS

### Brute

- Mechanical Translation: Heavy, sweeping strikes designed to shatter shields and break bones.
    
- 2d6 Application: When this creature wins a Clash as the Aggressor, it inflicts +1 Impact and forces the target back 1 square. If the target hits a wall or solid obstacle, they immediately take 1 Dissonant Stress from the concussive force.
    

### Vicious 

- Mechanical Translation: Jagged fangs, rusted serrated daggers, or disease-ridden claws that leave lingering wounds.
    
- 2d6 Application: If this creature inflicts damage on a player character, the target must immediately make a Prowess check. If they fail they gain the Bleeding condition. On their next Activation, they can spend 1 Momentum to "Steady the Wound".
    

### Swarm 

- Mechanical Translation: Small, overwhelming numbers of vermin or gibbering minor cultists attacking as one mind.
    
- 2d6 Application: (Fodder Only). These units do not occupy unique tactical slots. When multiple units with the Swarm trait engage the same PC, they pool their power. The primary attacker receives a flat +1 to their Clash roll for every additional Swarm unit adjacent to the target.

## TACTICAL & PSYCHOLOGICAL TRAITS

#### Terrifying 

- Mechanical Translation: A harrowing presence—whether an eldritch abomination or a faceless, silent headsman—that cracks the human mind.
    
- 2d6 Application: When a PC draws into an engagement with this creature or it activates within line of sight, the PC must immediately roll a Will + Wits check against TN 8.
    

- Failure: The PC immediately takes 1 Dissonant Stress and suffers a -1 penalty to their next Clash check as panic impairs their vision.
    

#### Cunning Leader 

- Mechanical Translation: A ruthless commander or pack alpha who reads the battlefield with chilling tactical precision.
    
- 2d6 Application: At the beginning of the Round during the Draw phase, this creature can pass its own high Reflex attribute down to any allied Fodder unit within its line of sight, allowing the minions to strike with unexpected coordination. Additionally, whenever an ally within its line of sight dies, this creature gains 1 Momentum out of pure malice or tactical adaptation.
    

#### Unstable Volatility

- Mechanical Translation: A creature bloated with volatile arcane radiation, alchemical compounds, or demonic instability.
    
- 2d6 Application: If this creature is struck by an attack that had exploded (6s), or if it rolls Snake Eyes (fumbles) on its own action, its containment ruptures. All characters (allies and enemies alike) within a 2-meter radius must defend against an immediate burst of raw energy, taking 2 points of Locked Stress (if magical) or Dissonant Stress (if alchemical/fire).

### How to build a "Gutter Beast" (Elite Skirmisher)

To see how easily this deploys, let’s combine a baseline Tier 2 Elite template with this manifest:

- Baseline Stats: Prowess +2, Reflex +2, Wits +1, Will +0. Stress Limit: 4. Base Impact: 2.
    
- Traits Added: Skittering + Vicious
    

Tactical Persona at the Table: When the Draw happens, the Gutter Beast uses its Skittering trait to grab a +1 to initiative. It acts as an aggressive harrier. It leaps into combat, slashes a player, and applies Vicious—putting the player on an immediate clock to spend Momentum or succumb to a death spiral of Dissonant Stress. If the players swing back and miss, the Beast wins the Clash as a Reactor and uses Skittering to immediately slip into the shadows without taking a parting blow.
