# MONSTER ENTITY TAGS (Breaking the Rules)

To make non-human enemies feel truly terrifying, you give them unique biological Tags rather than inflating their stats. Here are a few examples:

- Massive: This creature is too large for normal weapons to cause structural trauma. Weapons without the Sunder, Brutal, or Piercing tags automatically have their Impact halved before comparing to Wound Threshold.
    
- Terrifying: At the start of combat, any character looking at this creature must make a Will + Resolve check. On a failure, they automatically start the fight with 1 Stress.
    
- Regeneration: At the start of this creature's turn, it instantly clears 1 Wound Slot or 2 Stress. This trait is deactivated for the rest of the scene if the creature is hit with Fire or Acid.
    
- Amorphous: (e.g., Slimes, Swarms). This creature is immune to the Engagement Draw. It is always the Aggressor unless the player spends 1 Momentum to intercept it. Cannot be Grappled, Disarmed, or knocked Prone.
    

#### The Undead (Zombies, Skeletons, Vampires)

- Immunities: Immune to the Poisoned, Bleeding, and Fatigued conditions. They have no stamina, so they never take Stress penalties.
    
- Mundane Resistance: Mundane physical weapons (steel swords, arrows) have their total Impact reduced by 2 before comparing to the Undead's Wound Threshold.
    
- The Weakness: Weapons with the Blessed or Consecrated tag completely ignore this resistance, and Faith magic deals +2 Impact against them.
    
- Eldritch Aura: This creature warps the probability around it. It constantly projects The Mimicry Hex. Any character engaged with this creature treats natural 6s as 2s.
#### Ethereal / Incorporeal (Ghosts, Wraiths, Specters)

- Immunities: Completely immune to all mundane kinetic weapons. A steel sword swings right through them.
    
- The Weakness: They can only be harmed by Arcana, Faith, or weapons specifically coated in rare Alchemical oils (Ghost-bane) or Blessed by a Cleric.
    

#### Demonic / Infernal (Imps, Hellhounds, Arch-Demons)

- Aura of Cinders: Immune to Fire. Any character who successfully hits a Demon with a Melee Aggressor action takes 1 Stress from the ambient heat and jagged barbs on their skin.
    
- The Weakness: Holy Water (Alchemy) or Accursed Strike (Faith).
    

#### The Swarm (Rat packs, Spider broods)

- Amorphous
    
- Kinetic Resistance: Single-target weapons (daggers, arrows, spears) can never inflict a Wound on a Swarm, only Stress.
    
- The Weakness: They take double Stress from weapons with the Cleave or Sweep tags, and any Area of Effect magic (like a Fireball) completely ignores their Threshold, causing an automatic Wound.
    

  

#### The Lycanthrope / Unnatural Hide (Werewolves, Jackalweres)

The trope: Can only be killed by silver.

- Tag: Moon-Cursed
    
- The Mechanic: This creature converts all physical trauma into pure stamina drain. If a mundane weapon inflicts a Wound on this creature, the creature takes 1 Stress instead, and the Wound is completely ignored.
    
- The Weakness: Weapons coated in Alchemical Silver bypass this rule completely and automatically add +1 Impact to the strike, as the metal burns their blood.
    

#### The Acidic Terror (Slimes, Oozes, Rust Monsters)

The trope: Touching it destroys your gear.

- Tag: Corrosive Form
    
- The Mechanic: This creature does not use the Engagement Draw; it is always the Aggressor. Furthermore, any non-magical melee weapon that successfully strikes this creature immediately loses 1 Power for the rest of the adventure as the acid/rust eats the edge. If the creature wins a Clash against a Reactor with a Shield, the Shield's SV is permanently reduced by 1.
    
- The Weakness: Blunt weapons (Hammers/Maces) don't lose Power. Mages are the ultimate counter here, as Spells have no physical form to melt.
    

#### The Deadly Gaze (Basilisks, Gorgons)

The trope: Looking at it turns you to stone.

- Tag: Petrifying Gaze
    
- The Mechanic: At the start of this creature's turn, any character within 30 feet who is looking at it must make a Wits + Resolve check. Failure inflicts 1 Stress and the Sluggish condition (Disadvantage on Reflex rolls) as their joints begin to calcify. If a player maxes out their Stress from this gaze, they don't take a standard Wound—they turn entirely to stone.
    
- The Weakness: A player can declare they are fighting with their eyes closed or looking away. They are immune to the gaze, but suffer the Blinded condition (Disadvantage on Clashes) while doing so.
    

#### The Pack Hunters (Goblins, Kobolds, Dire Wolves)

The trope: Weak alone, lethal in a swarm.

- Tag: Mob Tactics
    
- The Mechanic: In Iron & Marrow, outnumbering an enemy normally grants a +1 Swarm Bonus per extra ally. Creatures with this tag are hyper-coordinated; their Swarm Bonus is +1 per extra ally on all Clashes.(breaks the max +2 cap).
    

#### The Vampire's Bite (Vampires, Blood Mages, Leeches)

The trope: They heal by draining your life.

- Tag: Blood-Drinker
    
- The Mechanic: Whenever this creature successfully inflicts a Wound on a living target with a melee attack or grapple, the surge of stolen life-force allows it to instantly heal 1 of its own Wound slots or clear all of its accumulated Stress.
    
- The Weakness: They cannot drain Undead or Constructs. Also, if the target is Poisoned when bitten, the Vampire suffers the poison's effects instantly.
    

#### The Troll's Flesh (Trolls, Hydras)

The trope: You have to burn it to kill it.

- Tag: Rapid Regeneration
    
- The Mechanic: At the start of this creature's turn, its flesh visibly knits back together. It instantly clears 1 Wound Slot and 1 Stress. A Troll can literally regenerate from a severed head if left alone for a minute.
    
- The Weakness: If the creature takes Wounds or Stress from Fire or Acid (like an Arcane caster's Elemental Shift wand, or an Alchemical Fire flask), the regeneration is completely deactivated for 1d4 turns.
