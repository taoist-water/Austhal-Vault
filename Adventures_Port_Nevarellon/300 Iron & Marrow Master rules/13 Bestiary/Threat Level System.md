# Threat Level System

Because Iron & Marrow uses a highly tactical engine (tracking Wounds, Stress penalties, and Momentum), you as the GM cannot be expected to track a full character sheet for every single bandit or goblin on the board. The game would grind to a halt.

To keep the game fast, brutal, and realistic, we will use a Threat Level Template System. Enemies are broken down into four distinct tiers, which instantly tell you how much math you need to track.

Here is the master framework for building adversaries:

### TIER 1: FODDER (Goblins, Starving Bandits, Skeletons)

Fodder exist to make the players feel like heroes and to drain their initial resources before the real fight begins.

- Stats: +0 in their primary Skill/Attribute, -1 in everything else.
    
- Wounds: 1 Wound Slot. (Any hit that beats their Threshold kills them instantly).
    
- Stress: 0 Stress Limit. (If an attack, spell, or Feint inflicts even 1 Stress on Fodder, they immediately break, flee, surrender, or pass out).
    
- GM Note: You do not track anything for Fodder. They are either alive, or they are off the board. This makes "Sweep" attacks and AoE magic incredibly satisfying.
    

### TIER 2: GRUNTS (Veteran Guards, Orc Raiders, Cultists)

The standard enemies of the world. Dangerous if they outnumber the players, as they utilize the Swarm Bonus (+1 to Engagement and Clash rolls per extra ally).

- Stats: +1 or +2 in their core competency (e.g., Prowess + Melee), +0 in others.
    
- Wounds: 2 Wound Slots.
    
- Stress Limit: 2. (They take a -1 penalty at 1 Stress, and convert to Wounds if pushed past 2).
    
- GM Note: They require mild tracking, but still fall relatively quickly once the players start applying Stress penalties.
    

### TIER 3: ELITES (Troll Brutes, Vampire Spawn, Enemy Knights)

Built using the exact same rules as the Player Characters. They are lethal 1-on-1 rivals.

- Stats: +2 or +3 in their core areas.
    
- Wounds: 3 Wound Slots.
    
- Stress Limit: Calculated normally (2 + Wits x 2).
    
- Special: Elites generate and spend Momentum just like players. They also possess 1 or 2 Feats (like Juggernaut or The Vanguard).
    

### TIER 4: BOSSES (Dragons, Lich Kings, Arch-Demons)

In a realistic game, a human cannot trade blows with a Dragon. Bosses break the structural rules of the game.

- Stats: Can exceed the +3 human cap (e.g., a Giant might have Prowess +5).
    
- The "Phase" Mechanic: Instead of standard Wounds, Bosses have Phases.
    

- Example: A Dragon has 3 Phases. Each Phase has 3 Wound Slots. When the players inflict 3 Wounds, the Dragon doesn't die—it loses a Phase, roars, clears all its Stress penalties, and changes its tactics (e.g., takes to the air).
    

- Special: Bosses have a fixed pool of "Dark Momentum" they can spend to interrupt player turns or ignore Stress penalties.
#### EXAMPLE STAT BLOCK: The Frost-Cave Troll (Elite / Tier 3)

Let's see how this looks in a clean, GM-friendly stat block:

THE FROST-CAVE TROLL

- Tier: Elite
    
- Stats: Prowess 3, Reflex 1, Wits -1, Will 1. (Melee 2, Athletics 2).
    
- Derived: Wound Threshold: 10 (4 + 3 Prowess + 3 Thick Hide). Stress Limit: 2 (Low intelligence).
    
- Weapons: * Uprooted Tree Trunk (2H): Power 3, Reach, Brutal.
    

- Claws (1H): Power 1.
    

- Tags: * Regeneration (Fire deactivates). * Massive.
    
- Momentum: Starts with 2.
    
- Tactics: The Troll has high Prowess but terrible Wits. Players should avoid trying to beat its Wound Threshold of 10 with physical attacks. Instead, mages should hit it with Fire to stop the Regeneration, and rogues should use tricks/feints to hit its low Stress Limit, turning its attacks into fumbles.
