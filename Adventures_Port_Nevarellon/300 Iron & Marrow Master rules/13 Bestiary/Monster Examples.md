# Monster Examples
####  The Goblin Scrapper (FODDER)

Fodder enemies die in one hit and flee easily. They rely on numbers.

- Wound Threshold: 5 (Base 4 + 0 Prowess + 1 Leather)
    
- Wounds: 1 (Any hit that beats their threshold kills them)
    
- Stress Limit: 0 (They don't use Stress; if a mechanic forces them to take Stress, they break and run)
    
- Primary Attack (Rusty Shiv): 2d6 + 0.
    
- Special: Pack Tactics: Gains a +1 to hit if another goblin is engaging the same target.
    

#### 2. The Orc Raider (GRUNT)

Standard, dangerous frontline combatants.

- Wound Threshold: 7 (Base 4 + 2 Prowess + 1 Thick Hide)
    
- Wounds: 2
    
- Stress Limit: 2
    
- Primary Attack (Greataxe): 2d6 + 3 (Rolling Prowess +2 and Melee +1)
    
- Maneuvers: Has access to Power Attack (-2 hit, +2 Impact).
    

#### 3. The Bandit Captain (ELITE)

A serious threat that requires party synergy to defeat.

- Wound Threshold: 9 (Base 4 + 2 Prowess + 3 Breastplate)
    
- Wounds: 3
    
- Stress Limit: 4
    
- Primary Attack (Masterwork Halberd): 2d6 + 5 (Rolling Prowess +2, Melee +2, Masterwork +1)
    
- Maneuvers: Has access to Precise Strike (bypasses Armor) and Battlefield Orator (Influence skill to rally Grunts).

### EXAMPLE STAT BLOCK: The Frost-Cave Troll (Elite / Tier 3)

Let's see how this looks in a clean, GM-friendly stat block:

THE FROST-CAVE TROLL

- Tier: Elite
    
- Stats: Prowess 3, Reflex 1, Wits -1, Will 1. (Melee 2, Athletics 2).
    
- Derived: Wound Threshold: 10 (4 + 3 Prowess + 3 Thick Hide). Stress Limit: 2 (Low intelligence).
    
- Weapons: * Uprooted Tree Trunk (2H): Power 3, Reach, Brutal.
    

- Claws (1H): Power 1.
    

- Tags: * Regeneration (Fire deactivates). * Massive.
    
- Momentum: Starts with 2.
    
- Tactics: The Troll has high Prowess but terrible Wits. Players should avoid trying to beat its Wound Threshold of 10 with physical attacks. Instead, mages should hit it with Fire to stop the Regeneration, and rogues should use tricks/feints to hit its low Stress Limit, turning its attacks into fumbles.

## 1. THE BLOOD-GORGED JOTUNN (The Giant)

A towering, misshapen brute of muscle and scarred flesh. It smells of old blood and rancid tallow, wielding a tree trunk studded with rusted chariot wheels.

- Threat Level: Boss (Tier 4)
    
- Scale: Huge (Scale +2)
    
- Attributes: Prowess +5, Reflex -1, Wits -1, Will +2
    
- Derived Stats:
    

- Wound Threshold [WT]: 13 (4 Base + 5 Prowess + 4 Scale Adjustment)
    
- Wound Slots per Phase: 5 (4 Base + 1 Scale Adjustment)
    
- Stress Limit: 0 (2 + [-1 Reflex x 2]) — Extremely easy to throw off balance or blind with tactical actions.
    

- Dark Momentum Pool: 3
    

### ⚖️ Scale Modifiers (Huge vs. Human)

- Easy Target: Humans gain a flat +2 bonus to their Clash checks when attacking the Giant.
    
- Overwhelming Force: Humans attempting to Block the Giant's club take 1 automatic Dissonant Stress even on a success. Because the Giant is two sizes larger, human Block actions suffer Disadvantage (roll 3d6, keep lowest two).
    

### 🛠️ Manifest Traits

- Brute (Trait 4): When the Giant wins a Clash as the Aggressor, it inflicts +1 Impact and hurls the target back 1 square. If they slam into a wall, they take 1 Dissonant Stress.
    
- Hardy (Trait 1): The Giant can spend 1 Dark Momentum to Soak damage, reducing incoming Impact by 2 per point spent instead of 1.
    
- Sweeping Club (Unique Action): Spend 1 Momentum. The Giant swings its weapon in a devastating arc. It makes a single Prowess check targeted against all characters occupying an entire adjacent zone.
    

### 🌀 The Phase Loop (2 Phases)

- Phase 1 (The Butcher): Uses its Sweeping Club to control the battlefield, keeping characters at bay.
    
- Phase 2 (Primal Rage): Triggered when its first 5 Wound slots fill. The Giant roars, clears its stress penalties, and shatters its club. It loses Sweeping Club but gains +1 Reflex and can now use its Action to pick up a Scale 0 character (Human) and throw them at another character as a ranged attack (Prowess vs Reflex).

## 2. THE ASH-BLIGHT DRAKE (The Dragon)

An ancient, calculating apex predator whose scales are fused with volcanic slag. It does not fight out of anger, but with a cruel, cold intelligence.

- Threat Level: Boss (Tier 4)
    
- Scale: Gargantuan (Scale +3)
    
- Attributes: Prowess +6, Reflex +2, Wits +2, Will +4
    
- Derived Stats:
    

- Wound Threshold [WT]: 16 (4 Base + 6 Prowess + 6 Scale Adjustment)
    
- Wound Slots per Phase: 6 (4 Base + 2 Scale Adjustment)
    
- Stress Limit: 6 (2 + [2 Reflex x 2])
    

- Dark Momentum Pool: 3
    

### ⚖️ Scale Modifiers (Gargantuan vs. Human)

- Immunity to Petty Steel: Standard Scale 0 infantry weapons (swords, spears, standard bows) cannot inflict Wounds on the Drake, only Stress. To inflict actual physical Wounds, players must use weapons with the Heavy or Siege tags (ballistas, heavy explosive jars, two-handed war-picks leveraging momentum).
    
- Massive Disadvantage: Humans attempting to Block or Dodge the Drake’s physical strikes suffer Disadvantage.
    

### 🛠️ Manifest Traits

- Plated (Trait 2): Reduces all incoming standard Impact damage by a flat -1. Bypassed only if a player rolls a Perfect Form (natural 3 and 4) or an Ace (exploding 6).
    
- Terrifying (Trait 7): Any character drawing into an engagement with the Drake must pass a Will + Wits check (TN 8). On a failure, they immediately take 1 Dissonant Stress and suffer a -1 penalty to their next Clash check.
    
- Ash-Blight Breath (Unique Action): Spend 2 Momentum. The Drake exhales a choking cloud of superheated ash and cinder into a zone. All characters in that zone must roll Reflex + Dodge vs TN 10.
    

- Failure: 3 Impact (Heavy damage).
    
- Success: 2 Dissonant Stress as they narrowly dive behind cover.
    

### 🌀 The Phase Loop (3 Phases)

- Phase 1 (Grounded Tyrant): The Drake fights on the ground, using its tail and claws, preserving its momentum to Soak incoming siege fire.
    
- Phase 2 (The Winged Calamity): Upon losing its first Phase, it takes to the air. It can now only be targeted by Ranged weapons or players who scale the terrain. It uses its activation to rain down Ash-Blight Breath.
    
- Phase 3 (Molten Core): Stripped of its armor scales, its exposed flesh glows with white-hot magma. It lands, moving with erratic desperation. It loses Plated, but its baseline Prowess increases to +7, and Ash-Blight Breath now costs only 1 Momentum to deploy.

## 3. THE SINK-HOLE LEVIATHAN (The Kraken)

An ancient, multi-limbed nightmare lurking in coastal abysses or drowned ruins. It breaks ships like dry twigs, operating less like a single creature and more like an entire environmental hazard.

- Threat Level: Boss (Tier 4)
    
- Scale: Gargantuan (Scale +3)
    
- Attributes: Prowess +7, Reflex +1, Wits +0, Will +3
    
- Derived Stats:
    

- Wound Threshold [WT]: 17 (4 Base + 7 Prowess + 6 Scale Adjustment)
    
- Wound Slots per Phase: 6 (4 Base + 2 Scale Adjustment)
    
- Stress Limit: 4 (2 + [1 Reflex x 2])
    

- Dark Momentum Pool: 4
    

### ⚖️ Scale Modifiers (Gargantuan vs. Human)

- The Maw Untouchable: The Kraken’s main body is submerged or out of reach. Standard weapons cannot wound it directly. Players must combat its individual limbs to force it to surface.
    

### 🛠️ Manifest Traits

- Multi-Tentacled (Unique Mechanical Variant): The Kraken spawns 4 autonomous Tentacles onto the tactical map.
    

- Each Tentacle acts on its own Draw (Initiative) using the Kraken's Prowess (+7) to execute Clashes.
    
- Tentacles are treated as Scale 0 entities with 1 Wound Slot. Severing a tentacle (filling its wound slot) inflicts 1 automatic Wound to the Kraken's current Phase pool.
    

- Vicious Constriction (Trait 5 Variant): When a Tentacle wins a Clash as an Aggressor, it inflicts no damage but puts the target into a Grappled condition. At the start of their turn, a Grappled character must spend 1 Momentum or take 2 points of Dissonant Stress from the crushing pressure.
    
- Ink Murk (Reactionary Trait): Spend 1 Dark Momentum when a tentacle is severed. The Kraken expels a cloud of black, blinding bile. The local zone becomes heavily obscured. All player checks within or entering the zone suffer Disadvantage for 1 round.
    

### 🌀 The Phase Loop (3 Phases)

- Phase 1 (Hull Crusher): The tentacles lash out, focusing purely on smashing structures, capsizing boats, or destroying defensive barriers to strip away player cover.
    
- Phase 2 (Drag to the Deep): The tentacles change tactics, prioritizing Vicious Constriction. On the Kraken’s turn, any character currently Grappled is dragged 1 square closer to the center vortex.
    
- Phase 3 (The Maw Approaches): Having taken 12 total Wounds (or losing 12 tentacles), the massive, beak-lined head of the Leviathan surfaces directly in the central zone. The head can now be targeted by standard weapons. The Kraken loses its individual tentacle actions and instead gains a single, horrific biting attack: Prowess +7, 4 Impact (Heavy, Armour-Piercing).

### TIER 1: The Corpse-Trench Rat Brood

A writhing, starving mass that exists purely to drain the players' Momentum and Wounds before the real threat arrives.

- Tier: Fodder
    
- Scale: Small (-1)
    
- Stats: Reflex +1, Prowess -1, Wits -1, Will -1.
    
- Derived: Wound Threshold: 2 (4 - 1 Prowess - 1 Scale). Stress Limit: 0. Wound Slots: 1.
    
- Weapons:
    

- A Thousand Teeth: Power 1, Close-Quarters.
    

- Tags:
    

	- Amorphous / Swarm: Immune to the Engagement Draw (always the Aggressor unless intercepted by a player spending 1 Momentum).
    
	- Kinetic Resistance: Single-target weapons (daggers, arrows, spears) can never inflict a Wound on the Swarm, only Stress.
    
	- Mob Tactics: Their Swarm Bonus to Clashes is +1 per extra ally engaging the target, breaking the standard +2 cap.
    

- Tactics: Do not bother tracking them on a character sheet. They flood the grid. Because they are immune to physical Wounds from single-target weapons, the party's standard martial fighters will be overwhelmed. The players must use Area of Effect magic, weapons with the Cleave or Sweep tags (which deal double Stress to Swarms), or utilize Shove actions to survive the attrition.
    

### TIER 2: The Hollowed Hoplite

An animated husk of a forgotten empire's infantry. They do not feel pain, they do not tire, and they do not break formation.

- Tier: Grunt
    
- Scale: Standard (0)
    
- Stats: Prowess +2, Reflex +0, Wits -1, Will +0. (Melee +2, Block +2).
    
- Derived: Wound Threshold: 8 (4 + 2 Prowess + 2 Heavy Shield). Stress Limit: N/A (Undead). Wound Slots: 2.
    
- Weapons:
    

- Rusted Phalanx Spear: Power 2, Reach, Versatile.
    
- Bronze Tower Shield: SV 3, Heavy (Reduces MV by 10ft).
    

- Tags:
    

	- The Undead: Immune to Poisoned, Bleeding, and Fatigued. They have no stamina, so they never take Stress penalties.
    
	- Mundane Resistance: Mundane physical weapons (steel swords, arrows) have their total Impact reduced by 2 before comparing to the Hoplite's Wound Threshold.
    

- Tactics: A brutal roadblock. Because they do not take Stress penalties, tricks, feints, and intimidation are useless. With an effective WT of 10 against mundane weapons due to their resistance, players must flank them to avoid the SV 3 Tower Shield, or rely on Faith magic and weapons with the Consecrated tag (which completely ignore Undead resistance and add +2 Impact).
    

### TIER 3: The Charnel Hound

A massive, heavily muscled predator born of the abyss. It heals its own trauma by ripping the life from its prey.

- Tier: Elite
    
- Scale: Large (+1)
    
- Stats: Prowess +3, Reflex +2, Wits +1, Will +1. (Athletics +2, Notice +2).
    
- Derived: Wound Threshold: 9 (4 + 3 Prowess + 2 Scale). Stress Limit: 4 (2 + Wits x 2). Wound Slots: 3.
    
- Weapons:
    

- Crushing Jaws: Power 2, Sunder.
    
- Barbed Claws: Power 1, Close-Quarters.
    

- Tags:
    

	- Demonic / Infernal (Aura of Cinders): Immune to Fire. Any character who successfully hits the Hound with a Melee Aggressor action takes 1 Stress from the ambient heat.
    
	- Blood-Drinker: Whenever this creature successfully inflicts a Wound on a living target, it instantly heals 1 of its own Wound slots or clears all of its accumulated Stress.
    

- Momentum: Starts with 2.
    
- Tactics: As an Elite, it uses Momentum just like the players. It will aggressively Charge to guarantee the Engagement Draw win, relying on its Sunder jaws to crack heavily armored fighters. Melee characters are punished just for hitting it due to the Aura of Cinders. The party must focus on Ranged attacks or use Holy Water / Faith magic to exploit its weakness.
    

### TIER 4: The Cypress Lich (The Woven King)

A sovereign of rot and corrupted nature. Reality itself bends and rusts in its presence.

- Tier: Boss
    
- Scale: Huge (+2)
    
- Stats: Prowess +2, Reflex +1, Wits +4, Will +4. (Arcana +3).
    
- Derived: Wound Threshold: 10 (4 + 2 Prowess + 4 Scale). Stress Limit: 10 (2 + Wits x 2).
    
- The Phase Mechanic: 3 Phases. Each Phase has 4 Wound Slots (3 base + 1 from Huge scale).
    
- Weapons:
    

- Grave-Dirt Slam: Power 3, Reach, Blunt.
    
- Master Arcana: (Elemental Burst, Disintegrate).
    

- Tags:
    

	- Terrifying: At the start of combat, any character looking at it must roll Will + Resolve or start the fight with 1 Stress.
    
	- Massive: Weapons without Sunder, Brutal, or Piercing have their Impact halved before comparing to its WT 10.
    
	- Eldritch Aura: The Lich passively projects The Mimicry Hex. Any character engaged with this creature treats a natural 6 on their dice as a 2.
    

- Dark Momentum: Pool of 5 per Phase.
    
- Tactics: The Eldritch Aura completely turns off the players' ability to roll a Desperate Edge (exploding 6s), stripping away their highest damage potential. The party must rely on the Heavy Hitter tag (which treats 6s as 7s rather than exploding) or perfectly calculated Momentum spends to break its Wound Threshold. When the players finally deplete its first 4 Wound Slots, it loses a Phase, clears all Stress, roars, and fundamentally changes its tactics for the next phase.
