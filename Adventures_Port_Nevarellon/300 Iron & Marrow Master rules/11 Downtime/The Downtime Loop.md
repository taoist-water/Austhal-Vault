# The Downtime Loop

Between primary narrative engagements or missions, characters enter a period of relative safety (a village, a walled city, a hidden encampment). This phase plays out in three clean steps:

1. Reset Momentum: All characters' Momentum pools drop to 0 (unless carrying a trait/feat that allows them to preserve an initial point).
    
2. Pay Upkeep: Characters must spend localized coin or resources to survive. Failing to pay upkeep forces the character to start the next adventure with 1 automatic Dissonant Stress from malnutrition or exposure.
    
3. Allocate Endeavours: Based on the length of the narrative gap, the GM grants players 1, 2, or 3 Endeavour Slots to execute actions from the catalog below.

## The Downtime Resolution Ladder

All downtime tests are rolled against TN 8 (2d6 + Attribute + Skill). The results are compared to the following scale to determine the success;

- Snake Eyes (Natural 2): Catastrophic Blunder. The action backfires severely, inflicting permanent harm or resource loss.
    
- Failure (Total < 8): Wasted Effort. Time and materials are lost; no benefits gained.
    
- Success (Total 8–11): Standard Return. The basic effect of the Endeavour is achieved.
    
- Massive Success (Total 12+): Exceptional Return. The character over-performs, gaining secondary benefits or saving resources.
