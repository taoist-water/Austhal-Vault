# Cosmology: The Great Fracture & The Infinite Disk
#lore/cosmology #lore/history #status/solid

> "We killed the anchors of the world to steal their sky. Now we drift on a broken plate, looking out into nothing, while the ghosts of our creators scratch at the edges of reality to get their bones back." — Ancient Elven Fragment, Year Unknown

---

## 🌌 The Primordial State: The Sphere
Before history began, existence was a perfect, closed, three-dimensional system—The Sphere. 
- There was no beginning, no end, and no decay. It was a flawless perpetual motion machine of reality.
- **The Creators:** Immense, nameless entities built this closed system. For them, the creation and maintenance of the Sphere was their ultimate purpose. 
- **The Experiment:** To test the limits of this infinite loop, the entities created the four mortal races—Humans, Elves, Dwarves, and Halflings—each entity designing a variant to see if any mortal could find a hidden beginning or end within the eternal cycle.

---

## 🩸 The Corruption of Individuation & The Naming
A catastrophic, unforeseen byproduct occurred when mortal life was introduced: **Individuation**.
As mortals began to experience distinct, separate lives, their consciousness fractured away from the unity of the universe. 

- **The Virus of Emotion:** This fragmentation birthed petty, volatile mortal emotions (greed, envy, ambition, fear). Because the creators were linked to their creations, this emotional rot bled upward.
- **The Naming:** The mortals began to name their creators. In this naming, they further infected the entities with **Identity**. Petty mortal Ego and Id shaped the nameless creators into the narrow visions of the mortal races. They became selfish, prideful, cruel, loving, and hateful—everything the mortals found in themselves. 
- **The Divide of Death:** For the first time, the entities fractured from the great oneness and became a pantheon of many. Anarchy bloomed. The Gods realized they were eternal within the Sphere, but the Mortals realized they were finite—each new mortal was unique, and when they died, they ceased to exist.

---

## 🔨 The Deicide & The Shattering
Driven by a desire for absolute freedom, immortality, and power, the mortals stole the magic and science of their creators. They weaponized this knowledge to execute a cosmic rebellion. The weight of this deicide tore the fabric of reality apart.

- **The Great Disk (The Material Plane):** The Sphere holding existence together collapsed. It was violently reshaped into a flat plane of physical matter so staggeringly vast that no known mortal has ever found its outer edge, nor dug deep enough to find the sky at the bottom.
- **The Void:** The surrounding infinity became an absolute nothingness in which the Disk drifts endlessly.
- **The Physical Remnants:** The Gods did not vanish; they were infinitely fractured. The Great Disk became the Material Plane, and scattered throughout its unfathomable depths are the physical, calcified remnants and shards of the murdered Gods.
