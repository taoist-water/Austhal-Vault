# Economy: The Price of Survival
#lore/economy #lore/society #status/solid

> "A dockhand bleeds for three coppers a day. The toll to cross into the High Quarter is five. The Council didn't need to build a wall around the slums; they just priced the gates out of reach." — Maeve the Scribe

---

## 💰 The Currency Standard
Port Nevarellon and the Whispering Coast operate on a strict, tri-metal fiat system enforced by the Council of Five. 

*   **Copper Pennies (cp):** The currency of the Un-Landed. Stamped with a crude anchor. *(10 cp = 1 sp)*
*   **Silver Pieces (sp):** The primary day-to-day trade currency used by merchants, guild-members, and mercenaries. Stamped with the scales of commerce. *(20 sp = 1 gs)*
*   **Gold Sovereigns (gs):** Heavy, pristine coins held only by high nobility, cartels, and the Council. They are rarely seen in the streets; they are used to buy ships, estates, and armies.

---

## 🔨 The Baseline of Labor (Income)
The economy of the city is designed as a poverty trap. Un-Landed laborers are paid just enough to survive the day, preventing them from ever saving enough to buy property or legally arm themselves.

*   **Unskilled Laborer (Dockhand, Mud-dredger, Timber-hauler):** **2 to 4 cp per day.** It is brutal, 14-hour work. If they get injured or fall sick, they earn nothing.
*   **Skilled Un-Landed Tradesman (Fish-gutter, Apprentice Rigger):** **5 to 8 cp per day.** 
*   **Landed Guild Artisan (Blacksmith, Shipwright):** **1 to 3 sp per day.**
*   **Golden Company Mercenary (Basic Grunt):** **3 sp per day**, plus guaranteed military rations and a dry cot (which is why recruitment is so appealing to desperate locals).

## 🍞 The Cost of Living (Expenses)
To understand the squalor of the Muddy Docks, one must look at what a laborer's 3 coppers actually buys them at the end of a shift:

*   **1 cp:** A bowl of thin, heavily salted fish-head stew and a chunk of hardtack bread.
*   **1 cp:** A mug of watered-down, marsh ale (despairing soles sometimes dissolve [[Cindin|Cindin]] into it numb the pain of the labor).
*   **1-2 cp:** Nightly rent for a lice-infested hammock strung up in a crowded, drafty warehouse (a "flophouse").
*   ***The Trap:*** A dockhand's daily survival costs exactly what they earn. There is zero margin for error. If they rip their boots or catch marsh-fever, they must take a loan from the [[The Iron-Anchor Syndicate|Syndicate]] just to eat.

---

## 🌉 The Oppression Economy (Taxes & Tolls)
The Council of Five uses specific, localized taxation to keep the poor physically and legally confined.

*   **The Vagrancy Tolls (The Invisible Walls):**
    *   To cross from the Muddy Docks into the Merchant Quarter: **1 cp** (A third of a day's wage just to walk to a better market).
    *   To cross into the High Quarter: **5 cp** (Mathematically impossible for a laborer to justify, keeping the elites completely isolated from the sight of poverty).
*   **The Guild-Tax:** If an Un-Landed citizen is caught selling a good (like a carved wooden toy or a caught fish) without a Landed Guild-Master's stamp, the fine is **1 sp (10 cp)**. If they can't pay, they are sent to the debt-prisons to work it off.
*   **The Golden Writ (The Peace-Bond):** The legal document required to carry a martial weapon costs **5 Gold Sovereigns (1,000 cp)**. It would take an unskilled laborer nearly a year of working every single day, without ever eating or paying rent, to afford the right to legally carry a sword.
