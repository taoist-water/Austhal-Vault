# Faction: The Iron-Anchor Syndicate
#faction/underground #status/solid

> "The City Watch keeps the peace in the plazas, but the Syndicate keeps the ships moving. Displace them, and the whole port starves in a fortnight." — Councilman Henderson

## 🏛️ Overview
- **Type:** Maritime Smuggling Ring / Extortion Syndicate / Labor Cartel
- **Headquarters:** [[000 Atlas/The Black Mast Warehouse|The Black Mast Warehouse]] (Located in the deep recess of the northern wharf)
- **Current Leader:** [[200 Cast/Garrick the Keelhauler|Garrick the Keelhauler]]]
- **Primary Influence:** Absolute control over [[000 Atlas/The Muddy Docks|The Muddy Docks]] and the outer shipping lanes; deep financial tendrils inside the city's merchant guilds.

## 💰 Logistics & Economics (The Realism Anchor)
- **Sources of Income:**
  - **"The Anchor Tax":** A mandatory protection fee levied on every independent merchant vessel docking in the lower slips.
  - **Labor Monopoly:** Total control over the Stevedores & Dockworkers Union. If a ship captain refuses to pay, their cargo is left to rot on deck.
  - **Alchemical Fencing:** Smuggling raw, unstable mineral components like [[300 Mechanics/Sun-Iron|Sun-Iron]] out of the mountains to unlicensed alchemists in the city.
- **Expenses & Upkeep:**
  - Massive monthly bribes to high-ranking captains of [[100 Society/The City Watch|The Blue-Cloak Watch]] to look the other way.
  - Wages for roughly 300 "Enforcers" (grizzled sailors, thuggish dock hands, and crossbowmen).
  - Maintenance on a fleet of six low-profile, fast-moving coastal cutters used for nighttime off-loading.
- **Primary Assets:** Key drydocks, waterfront warehouses with hidden tidal cellars, and a vast network of debt ledgers holding leverage over local politicians.

## ⚖️ Leverage & Power Dynamics
- **Political Leverage:** They deliberately keep the docks functional. By suppressing petty street crime and ensuring foreign cargo is unloaded efficiently, they keep the city's trade economy stable. The ruling elite tolerate them because the alternative—unorganized chaos and dock strikes—would bankrupt the city.
- **Monopoly / Unique Value:** They control the pilot-boats. The entrance to Port Nevarellon's harbor is a treacherous maze of jagged shoals. Without a Syndicate guide-boat to lead large merchant ships in, foreign vessels face catastrophic shipwrecks.
- **Vulnerabilities:** Heavy reliance on food imports. If a rival power or an honest military faction blockaded the landward gates of the city, the Syndicate's baseline street muscle would starve and riot within weeks.

## ⚡ Internal Friction & Conflict
- **Internal Factions:** A growing rift exists between the *Old Guard* (who want to stick to traditional smuggling and protection) and the *Young Bloods* led by [[200 Cast/Silas Bane|Silas Bane]], who wants to violently challenge [[100 Society/The High Alchemist Guild|The High Alchemist Guild]] for control of the bioluminescent lighting monopoly.
- **External Rivals:** [[100 Society/The Tidespoken Clergy|The Tidespoken Clergy]] (who actively undermine Syndicate recruitment by feeding and protecting the poorest dockworkers).
- **Public Perception:** Feared by the merchants, loathed by the nobility, but viewed by many impoverished dockworkers as a necessary shield against the tyrannical taxes of the city's crown.

## 👥 Notable Members
- [[200 Cast/Garrick the Keelhauler|Garrick the Keelhauler]] — *The Grandmaster. A ruthless but pragmatic former privateer.*
- [[200 Cast/Silas Bane|Silas Bane]] — *Enforcer Captain. Ambitious, volatile, and heavily invested in alchemical weapons.*
- [[200 Cast/Maeve the Scribe|Maeve the Scribe]] — *The Bookkeeper. A brilliant woman who tracks the bribes and smuggling manifests from a hidden office in [[000 Atlas/The Drowned Rat Tavern|The Drowned Rat Tavern]].*

## 🤫 Shared Backstory & The Core Secret 
Originally from the squalid farming villages outside the city, the sisters arrived in Port Nevarellon twenty years ago with nothing. They survived by converting a derelict ship hull into the port's most secure lounge.

The sisters do not just collect secrets for survival—they are running a massive extortion ring against the city's elite. Hidden behind a false bulkhead in their private cabin is a leather-bound journal written in Clara's precise code. It contains the names of several prominent nobles from **The High Quarter** who are secretly financing illegal smuggling operations through **The Iron-Anchor Syndicate** to avoid paying the [[Council of Five]]'s maritime tariffs. This ledger is their ultimate life insurance policy; if either sister is murdered, a designated courier has instructions to drop the journal directly on the steps of the High Court.

# Character: Garrick "The Keelhauler"
#cast/active #status/solid

> "A pirate hangs from the gibbet because he wants to steal a ship. Garrick dines on silver plate because he chose to buy the harbor." — Archon Sterling, High Quarter Court

## 📊 Vital Statistics
- **Full Name / Aliases:** Garrick Vance / "The Keelhauler" (A moniker from his mutinous privateer days that he now finds unrefined but useful for intimidation).
- **Current Occupation:** Grandmaster of [[100 Society/The Iron-Anchor Syndicate|The Iron-Anchor Syndicate]] / Unofficial Magistrate of the Waterfront.
- **Social Class / Standing:** Gilded Criminal / Industrial Oligarch.
- **Primary Residence:** The Captain's Cabin, a reinforced upper suite within [[000 Atlas/The Black Mast Warehouse|The Black Mast Warehouse]].
- **Affiliations:** [[100 Society/The Iron-Anchor Syndicate|The Iron-Anchor Syndicate]] (Supreme Leader); covertly linked to several members of [[000 Atlas/The High Quarter|The High Quarter]].

## ⚖️ Realism & Physicality
- **Age & Vitality:** 56 years old. His hair is the color of sea-foam, cropped short. Decades of exposure to salt, wind, and cheap rum have left his face deeply lined and leathery. He moves with a slow, deliberate gait that betrays severe osteoarthritis in his hips.
- **Physical Flaws / Limitations:** His left leg was crushed by a shifting cargo cannon twenty years ago; it healed poorly, leaving him with a pronounced limp that requires a heavy, iron-shod ash cane. He cannot sprint, and his left eye is clouded with a milky cataract, ruining his peripheral vision on that side.
- **Financial Status:** Imbued with immense, hidden wealth. He hoards foreign gold specie, title deeds to mainland farmland (his ultimate exit strategy), and high-grade alchemical contracts. He could buy a noble title tomorrow, but knows he would be assassinated within a week if he left his power base.
- **Equipment & Upkeep:** Dresses in bespoke, thick wool coats with tarnished silver buttons—practical for the damp cold of the docks, yet distinct from common deckhands. Carries a heavy, double-barreled flintlock pistol loaded with buckshot (reliable at short range where his vision fails) and his signature iron-capped cane.

## 🧠 Psychology & Drive
- **Immediate Goal:** Suppress the insubordination of his captain, [[200 Cast/Silas Bane|Silas Bane]], without starting a loud, bloody street war that would force the City Watch to crack down on the docks.
- **The Core Fear:** Becoming obsolete. He knows that if the city ever builds a freshwater river pipeline or bridges the shoals, his monopoly on harbor pilots and water distribution evaporates, and the Syndicate falls with it.
- **Moral Compromises:** Garrick views human life strictly as fuel for commerce. He has personally authorized the press-ganging of hundreds of vagrants, signed off on the systematic drowning of competitors, and maintains a ledger of synthetic debts to keep thousands of dockworkers bonded to his slips.

## 📜 Backstory & Current Role
Garrick began as a third-mate on an empire trade galley. When the crew mutinied against a tyrannical captain, Garrick didn't join the slaughter out of passion; he did it because he calculated that the ship's cargo of spices was worth more on the black market than ten years of navy wages. 

He didn't stay a pirate long. Recognizing that the men who sell the powder and buy the plunder make the real profit, he settled in Port Nevarellon. Over twenty years, he systematically  murdered, bribed, or consolidated the disparate boardwalk gangs into a single, corporate criminal entity: the Iron-Anchor Syndicate.

## 🤫 Current Conflict: The Shaking Anchor
Garrick’s greatest struggle is age. His body is failing, and his pragmatism is being misread as weakness by the younger generation. He spends more time analyzing trade sheets and balancing bribe ledgers than cracking skulls. He knows [[200 Cast/Silas Bane|Silas]] wants his seat, but Garrick is playing a longer game—he is currently negotiating with certain corrupt nobles to fully legitimize the Syndicate into an official "Maritime Logistics Guild," which would permanently shield his wealth under royal law.

## 🔗 Connected Notes
- **Subordinate / Threat:** [[200 Cast/Silas Bane|Silas Bane]] (Enforcer Captain plotting his removal)
- **Primary Financial Advisor:** [[200 Cast/Maeve the Scribe|Maeve the Scribe]] (The only person allowed to see his true ledger files)
- **Covert Partners:** [[200 Cast/The Dolly Sisters|The Dolly Sisters]] (He pays them for intelligence, completely unaware that they hold his secrets in their Black Ledger)
- **Command Center:** [[000 Atlas/The Black Mast Warehouse|The Black Mast Warehouse]]



# Character: Silas Bane

## 📊 Vital Statistics
- **Full Name / Aliases:** Silas Bane / "The Smoker" (Due to the lingering scent of sulfur and chemical reagents that follows him).
- **Current Occupation:** Enforcer Captain of [[100 Society/The Iron-Anchor Syndicate|The Iron-Anchor Syndicate]] / Controller of the Lower Slip Rackets.
- **Social Class / Standing:** Aggressive Street Aristocrat / Underworld Warlord.
- **Primary Residence:** A heavily guarded suite above the fighting pits at [[000 Atlas/The Rusty Anchor Foundry|The Rusty Anchor Foundry]].
- **Affiliations:** [[100 Society/The Iron-Anchor Syndicate|The Iron-Anchor Syndicate]] (Faction Leader of the Young Bloods); covert buyer from rogue members of [[100 Society/The High Alchemist Guild|The High Alchemist Guild]].

## ⚖️ Realism & Physicality
- **Age & Vitality:** 29 years old. Tall, broad-shouldered, and radiating an erratic, nervous energy. Unlike Garrick's slow deliberation, Silas moves with explosive, twitchy speed—a side-effect of chronic exposure to alchemical vapors.
- **Physical Flaws / Limitations:** His forearms and neck are permanently scarred with chemical chemical burns from a laboratory accident five years ago. He suffers from a recurring, dry alchemical cough that hacks up grey phlegm, and his fingers twitch rhythmically when he is forced to sit still for too long.
- **Financial Status:** Wealthy in terms of short-term liquid silver, which he spends recklessly on fine silk vests (deliberately shredded at the sleeves to show his scars), custom weaponry, and bribes to young dockhands. He keeps zero long-term investments or property deeds; his wealth is recycled directly into muscle and armaments.
- **Equipment & Upkeep:** Carries a custom, heavy-bladed boarding cutlass with a reinforced brass basket hilt. Hidden inside his coat are two volatile glass spheres filled with concentrated **Brine-Fire** (an unstable alchemical gel that ignites upon contact with salt water)—a dangerous, illegal weapon he procured through the black market.

## 🧠 Psychology & Drive
- **Immediate Goal:** Seize absolute control of the **Bioluminescent Lantern Monopolies** in the lower districts, weaponizing the resource against the upper city.
- **The Core Fear:** Being trapped beneath a ceiling. Silas despises the idea of a slow, pragmatic transition into "legitimacy." He genuinely believes that if the Syndicate tries to become a legal guild under the crown, the nobles will strip them of their true power and executioner's edge.
- **Moral Compromises:** Completely unhinged by cruelty. Where Garrick uses violence as a precise ledger correction, Silas uses it as performance art. He has ordered public flayings on the boardwalks, used experimental chemical agents on debtors, and treats his own street muscle as entirely disposable resources.

## 📜 Backstory & The Coup Strategy
Silas was born in the flooded alleys of [[000 Atlas/The Sunken Ward|The Sunken Ward]]. He climbed the ranks of the Syndicate by being the only enforcer crazy enough to board foreign warships to seize cargo. Garrick took him in as a blunt instrument, miscalculating that the boy's lack of macro-economic understanding would keep him subservient. 

Instead, Silas realized that muscle beats ledgers if you control the tools of destruction. 

### ⚙️ The Plan to Overthrow Garrick
Silas recognizes he cannot beat Garrick in a traditional corporate vote or political bribe war. His strategy relies on systemic destabilization:

1. **The Alchemical Monopoly:** Silas is secretly funding a rogue cell of alchemists to manufacture synthetic, concentrated bioluminescent fluid. He intends to sabotage the city's official supply lines, plunge the lower districts into total darkness, and then step forward as the *only* provider of light—forcing the Council to recognize his personal terms.
2. **The Scribe's Leverage:** Silas knows that [[200 Cast/Maeve the Scribe|Maeve]] holds Garrick's true financial ledgers. He is currently orchestrating a plan to kidnap or compromise Maeve, forcing her to alter the bribe records to make it look like Garrick is embezzling from his own ship captains.
3. **The Direct Strike:** Once the dock captains are rioting over the fake records and the lower districts are dark, Silas intends to use his Brine-Fire spheres to burn down [[000 Atlas/The Black Mast Warehouse|The Black Mast Warehouse]] with Garrick inside it, framing it as an accident caused by high-tide storms.

## ⚖️ Realism & Physicality
- **Age & Vitality:** 31 years old. Extremely pale from spending fourteen hours a day beneath sputtering tallow candles and dim bioluminescent globes. Her posture is slightly slouched from a decade of bending over heavy parchment ledgers.
- **Physical Flaws / Limitations:** Severely nearsighted; she requires a pair of rare, thick-lensed alchemical spectacles imported from the mainland to read fine script. Without them, she is virtually blind beyond an arm's length. Her fingers are permanently stained with deep black indelible gall-ink.
- **Financial Status:** Paid exceptionally well by Garrick, though her wealth is purely abstract—she has a massive "credit ledger" with the city's banks, but rarely leaves the warehouse to spend a single copper. Her true wealth lies in her ownership of the encrypted keys to every smuggling route on the coast.
- **Equipment & Upkeep:** Carries a custom leather writing kit containing varying grades of iron-gall ink, vellum scraping knives, and wax seals. Tucked into her bodice is a tiny, double-barreled brass derringer (loaded with lead balls)—useless at range, but meant for a last-resort desk ambush.

## 🧠 Psychology & Drive
- **Immediate Goal:** Keep the true financial bribe ledgers hidden from [[200 Cast/Silas Bane|Silas Bane]] while subtly falsifying just enough low-level manifest data to keep him from realizing she is stalling.
- **The Core Fear:** Being blinded or having her hands broken. She knows her entire value to the world is her mind and her eyes. If she is physically ruined, neither Garrick nor Silas will have any use for her, and her life will be forfeit.
- **Moral Compromises:** She actively doctors the death tolls and cargo loss reports of press-ganged workers, transforming human tragedies into clean, dry "operational deficits" to preserve the Syndicate's bottom line.

## 📜 Backstory & The Survival Rope
Maeve was born the illegitimate daughter of a disgraced high-quarter clerk. She inherited her father's mathematical mind but none of his status. After being arrested at age eighteen for forging merchant tax exemptions, [[200 Cast/Garrick the Keelhauler|Garrick]] recognized her genius, paid her massive execution bounty, and brought her to the docks to professionalize his disorganized extortion racket. For over a decade, she has been Garrick's shadow executive.

### ⚖️ The Balancing Act: Garrick vs. Silas
Maeve is currently trapped in a lethal vice:

*   **The Debt to Garrick:** She harbors a genuine, pragmatic loyalty to Garrick. He treats her with professional respect, keeps her safe, and pays her a premium. She knows that under Garrick's plan to legitimize into a legal guild, she would become a respected corporate treasurer.
*   **The Threat from Silas:** Silas has been subtly cornering her. He recently placed two of his own "Young Blood" enforcers as her permanent bodyguards outside her loft, ostensibly for her protection, but in reality, they are her jailers. Silas has made it terrifyingly clear: when the coup happens, she either hands over the modified ledger books to ruin Garrick's reputation, or her throat is cut.
*   **The Counter-Move:** To survive, Maeve has entered a secret pact with [[200 Cast/The Dolly Sisters|The Dolly Sisters]]. She is covertly passing duplicates of the Syndicate's most damning political secrets to Clara Dolly. If Silas kills her, Clara will release the *Black Ledger*, ensuring that the City Watch is forced to execute Silas and the entire Young Blood faction.

He frequently travels to the city's High Quarter to ensure the Council's maritime taxes continue to fund the protection of his duchy's borders, reminding them that if De Vonce falls to the wild inland, the city's trade will follow.
