# Settlement: Port Nevarellon
#location/city #status/solid

> "A sprawling, salt-encrusted maze of limestone plazas and rotting timber stilts, where the wealth of nations enters through the eastern slips and vanishes into the western slums." — Master Cartographer Vaelen

## 📊 City Profile
- **Type:** Maritime Trade Hub / Sovereign Port City
- **Estimated Population:** ~35,000 permanent residents (doubles during the spring trade season)
- **Governance:** [[Council of Five]] (A merchant oligarchy representing the city's primary industries)
- **Primary Export:** Refined salt, cured fish, imported spices, and smuggled alchemical goods.
- **Regional Location:** [[Whispering Coast]]

## 🪵 Urban Infrastructure & Logistics
To maintain a realistic setting, Port Nevarellon’s design is governed by its environment:
- **Fresh Water:** The city does *not* have a freshwater river. It relies entirely on massive stone cisterns located beneath the High Quarter that collect rainwater, and a single aqueduct pipe running from the foothills of [[The Jagged Spine]]. Water is a heavily taxed commodity.
- **Sanitation:** The Lower Districts have no sewage system; waste drains directly into the harbor via open stone gutters, leading to severe stagnation during low tides. The High Quarter uses a subterranean flush-vault system that vents out past the eastern cliffs.
- **Defense:** Shielded from ocean storms by a massive stone breakwater known as **The Sea-Wall**. Landward defenses consist of a 20-foot limestone curtain wall, though it is poorly maintained near the industrial sectors.

## 🏛️ The Six Districts
The city is starkly divided by geography, elevation, and wealth:

1. **[[000 Atlas/The High Quarter|The High Quarter]]** — Built on the northern limestone cliffs. Home to old nobility, the finest stonework, and the city's clean water vaults.
2. **[[000 Atlas/The Trade Plazas|The Trade Plazas]]** — The commercial heart. Cobblestone streets lined with banking houses, legal guilds, and upscale markets.
3. **[[000 Atlas/The Muddy Docks|The Muddy Docks]]** — A treacherous labyrinth of wooden boardwalks and ships permanently converted into housing. Heavily policed by gangs rather than the City Watch.
4. **[[000 Atlas/The Foundry Slips|The Foundry Slips]]** — The industrial sector. Dominated by shipyards, fish-curing smokehouses, and coal-fired alchemical refineries.
5. **[[000 Atlas/The Shades|The Shades]]** — A low-lying slum built on reclaimed marshland. It experiences mild flooding during spring tides, leaving the streets constantly slick with dark silt.
6. **[[000 Atlas/The Great Anchor Basin|The Great Anchor Basin]]** — The massive central harbor capable of hosting up to two hundred deep-sea vessels at once.

## ⚖️ Power & Law Enforcement
The balance of power in Port Nevarellon is delicate and highly transactional:
- **[[100 Society/The City Watch|The Blue-Cloak Watch]]**: The official military presence. They are well-equipped and professional in the High Quarter and Trade Plazas, but entirely corrupt or absent in the Docks and Sunken Ward.
- **The True Law**: Law enforcement in the lower districts is outsourced by pragmatism to [[100 Society/The Iron-Anchor Syndicate|The Iron-Anchor Syndicate]], who enforce their own harsh, unwritten rules to keep trade moving.

## 🔮 The Realism-Fantasy Intersect
While magic exists, it is bounded by industrial cost:
- **[[300 Mechanics/The Brine-Glow Lanterns| The Brine-Glow Lanterns:]]** The city's main avenues are illuminated at night not by oil, but by glass globes containing chemically preserved, bioluminescent deep-sea algae. Maintaining these lanterns is a massive municipal expense managed by [[100 Society/The High Alchemist Guild|The High Alchemist Guild]].
- **The Tide-Wards:** Ancient, eroding basalt monoliths are embedded along the Sea-Wall. They do not stop storms, but they stabilize the bedrock beneath the city to prevent the timber stilts from sliding into the ocean shelf during tremors.


# District: The Muddy Docks
#location/district #status/solid

> "Step carefully, keep your hand on your purse, and never look a press-ganger in the eye when the tide is low." — Common street warning

## 🏙️ District Profile
- **Settlement:** [[000 Atlas/Port Nevarellon|Port Nevarellon]]
- **Social Stratum:** Working Class, Transient Sailors, Squalid Dregs
- **Architecture & Infrastructure:** A massive, chaotic sprawl built almost entirely out of damp timber. Tenements and warehouses sit precariously on barnacle-encrusted stilts over the tidal mudflats. The walkways consist of buckled wooden boardwalks, rope bridges, and repurposed hulls of broken ships that act as permanent floating platforms.

## ⚖️ Law, Order & Safety
- **Guarding Presence:** The official [[100 Society/The City Watch|Blue-Cloak Watch]] refuses to patrol here after dusk, maintaining only a single, heavily fortified guard-post at the district's landward gate. 
- **Local Customs / Unwritten Rules:** Weapons must be kept bound or sheathed while on the main thoroughfares, but fighting with fists or rigging knives is largely ignored. To wear fine silks or flashy jewelry here is viewed as an invitation to be tossed into the mud and stripped.
- **Crime Level:** High/Violent. Assault, smuggling, illegal gambling, and forced maritime conscription (press-ganging) are daily realities.

## 🥖 Economy & Logistics
- **Primary Hubs:** [[000 Atlas/The Low-Tide Market|The Low-Tide Market]] (A chaotic trading bazaar that takes place directly on the exposed mud when the ocean recedes).
- **Condition of Streets/Sanitation:** Abysmal. There is absolutely no plumbing or stone drainage. All industrial runoff from upstream shipyards and raw waste from the tenements drops directly into the silt beneath the boardwalks. At low tide, the stench of stagnant sea-mud, rot, and cheap whale-oil fuel is overwhelming.
- **Key Resources/Utilities:** Fresh water must be carried in by wagon from the upper quarters and is sold by the barrel at exorbitant prices. Most residents survive on cheap, low-grade small beer or collected rainwater that tastes heavily of coal smoke.

## 📍 Key Locations & Sub-Locations
- [[000 Atlas/The Drowned Rat Tavern|The Drowned Rat Tavern]] — *Type: Waterfront Tavern & Smuggler Hub*
- [[000 Atlas/Slipway Seven|Slipway Seven]] — *Type: Commercial Drydock & Repair Yard*
- [[000 Atlas/The Brine-Glow Depot|The Brine-Glow Depot]] — *Type: Alchemical maintenance workshop for street lanterns*

## 👥 Power Players
- **Local Authority:** None officially. The city-appointed Harbor Master stays barricaded in his stone tower near the Trade Plazas.
- **Dominant Factions:** 
  - [[100 Society/The Iron-Anchor Syndicate|The Iron-Anchor Syndicate]] — *Controls the extortion rackets, shipping protection, and gambling dens.*
  - [[100 Society/The Tidespoken Clergy|The Tidespoken Clergy]] — *A religious sect that runs soup kitchens on the lower piers and keeps a fragile peace between the gangs.*

# Building: The Shades
#location/structure #status/solid

> "Don't let the velvet curtains fool you. The silk is damp, the wine is sour, and the women know exactly how much coin is left in your boots before you've even unbuckled your belt." — Overheard at the slips

## 📊 Quick Reference
- **Type of Establishment:** Waterfront Brothel, Gambling Den, and Information Exchange
- **District / Neighborhood:** [[000 Atlas/The Muddy Docks|The Muddy Docks]]
- **Owner / Proprietor:** [[200 Cast/The Dolly Sisters|The Dolly Sisters]] (Clara and Tessa Dolly)
- **Affiliation / Protection:** [[100 Society/The Iron-Anchor Syndicate|The Iron-Anchor Syndicate]] (Pays a heavy weekly tribute to ensure safety from religious zealots and rival gangs)

## 🪵 Architecture & Layout
- **Exterior Appearance:** Built out of a massive, double-decked merchant carrack hull that ran aground twenty years ago and was permanently built into the boardwalk infrastructure. The exterior timber is blackened with protective tar to resist sea-rot. Strands of cheap, faded violet silk hang over the portholes, and a tarnished iron lantern casting a dull red glow hangs above the gangplank entrance.
- **The Interior Layout:** 
  - **Public Area:** The main deck has been hollowed out into a dim, smoky lounge featuring a low-ceilinged bar and several curtained alcoves for drinking and dice games. The air is warm from two large iron coal braziers.
  - **Private / Restricted:** The lower decks (the old cargo hold) have been split into small, cramped cubicles divided by thick canvas and velvet drapes. Beneath the lowest deck lies the bilge—a damp, knee-deep crawlspace accessible via a trapdoor behind the bar, used to hide contraband or fleeing clients during a raid.

## 🦪 Sensory Details & Atmosphere
- **Smells:** Cheap perfume mixed with the sharp tang of vinegar (used to scrub down the linen), heavy tobacco smoke, damp bilge water, and the omnipresent scent of roasting chicory from the bar.
- **Sounds:** The rhythmic creaking of the ship's old hull as the tide shifts, the muffled clatter of bone dice on wood, and the low strumming of a rusted three-string lute in the corner.
- **Lighting:** Intentionally dark. Illumination relies on low-grade tallow candles and a few shuttered brass oil lamps that cast long, flickering shadows.

## 💰 Economics & Clientele
- **Regular Patrons:** Foreign merchant captains, local shipwrights, mid-level enforcers from the [[100 Society/The Iron-Anchor Syndicate|Syndicate]], and off-duty mercenaries looking to spend their coin quickly. 
- **Primary Income:** Premium alcohol markup, house cuts on private card games, and companionship. Unlike the rest of the docks, they strictly refuse payment in bartered goods or IOUs—they accept only solid silver, gold, or foreign specie.
- **Contraband / Side-Hustles:** The establishment acts as an informal "black bank." Ship captains who don't want to carry heavy chests of coin through the dangerous streets can deposit gold with the sisters in exchange for written, coded drafts accepted elsewhere in the docks.

## ⚔️ Security & Defense
- **Physical Security:** The gangplank is the only obvious way in or out, made of heavy iron-reinforced oak that can be winched up into the ship's hull from the inside if a riot breaks out on the boardwalks.
- **On-site Muscle:** [[200 Cast/Bruiser Ben|Bruiser Ben]], a hulking, silent former shipyard smith with a mangled hand, sits near the entrance. Two younger dockhands are paid to lounge near the back curtains with heavy ash-wood cudgels.
- **Escape Routes:** A small skiff is permanently tied to a hidden hatch in the stern of the hull, allowing someone to drop directly into the dark harbor water and row out into the basin unnoticed.
